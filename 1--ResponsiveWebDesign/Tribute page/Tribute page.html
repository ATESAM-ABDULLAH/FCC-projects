Field Marshal Muhammad Ayub Khan

(14 May 1907 – 19 April 1974)

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ayub Khan's life:

  • 14 May 1907 - Born near Larkana Pakistan.
  • July 1926 - Joined the Royal Military College, Sandhurst, as a trainee.
  • 10 April 1929 - Joined the 1/14th Punjab Regiment Sherdils, now known as the 5th Punjab Regiment.
  • 1947 - Promoted to brigadier and commanded a brigade in mountainous South Waziristan.
  • 1949 - Decorated with the Hilal-i-Jurat (HJ) by Prime Minister Liaquat Ali Khan for non-combatant service.
  • 17 January 1951 - Assumed charge as Commander-in-Chief of the Pakistan Army.
  • 24 February 1954 - Signed the Central Treaty Organization (CENTO) pact for Pakistan and his role in national politics.
  • 7 October 195 - Was appointed as chief martial law administrator (CMLA),then declared martial law.
  • 2 March 1961 - Passed and signed the "Muslim Family Laws".
  • 1963 - Signed the historic Sino-Pakistan Frontier Agreement with China despite US opposition.
  • 1966–67 - Wrestled with the United States's attempt to dictate Pakistan's foreign policy, while strengthening relations with the Soviet Union and China.
  • 1961–62 - Paid a state visit to the United Kingdom.
  • 19 April 1974 - Died of a heart attack on at his villa near Islamabad.
